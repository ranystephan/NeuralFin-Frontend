## Features

The News Page offer three main sections:

1. Company Data
2. Stock time series chart
3. Summarized News with Sentiment Analysis


## What it consists of

Click on a section below to learn about the different metrics that the page offers.

<div className="grid gap-4 mt-6">

<Card href="/docs/news/market-cap">

### Market Cap

The market capitalization of a company is the total value of the company's equity shares.

</Card>

<Card href="/docs/news/target-price">

### Target Price

The target price is the price at which an analyst believes a stock to be fairly valued.

</Card>

<Card href="/docs/news/yield">

### Dividend Yield

The dividend yield is the ratio of a company's annual dividend compared to its share price.

</Card>

<Card href="/docs/news/beta">

### Beta

Beta is a measure of the volatility, or systematic risk, of a security or portfolio in comparison to the market as a whole.

</Card>

<Card href="/docs/news/eps">

### Earnings Per Share

Earnings per share (EPS) is the portion of a company's profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock.

</Card>
<Card href="/docs/news/pe-ratio">

### PE Ratio

The price-to-earnings ratio (P/E ratio) is the ratio for valuing a company that measures its current share price relative to its per-share earnings.

</Card>

<Card href="/docs/news/ev-ebitda">

### EV to EBITDA

EV/EBITDA is a ratio that compares a company's Enterprise Value (EV) to its Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation & Amortization (EBITDA).

</Card>

<Card href="/docs/news/ev-revenue">

### EV to Revenue

EV/Revenue is a ratio that compares a company's Enterprise Value (EV) to its Revenue.

</Card>
<Card href="/docs/news/profit-margin">

### Profit Margin

Profit margin is a profitability ratio that measures how much profit a company makes per dollar of its revenue.

</Card>

</div>
